About Me

Mardoqueo Arteaga
Last updated in October 2020

Hi there!

I am an economist who has primarily worked in applied research roles thus far, including consumer marketing and enterprise risk research. I am also an active PhD student at Fordham University in my 3rd year with the research interests found on my home page, as well as an enthusiasm for computation and telling stories from data.

Much more important to me than what I do is why I do it and my atypical journey in the United States as an immigrant from El Salvador has been key in giving me perspective. My enthusiasm for economic research and desire to contribute to a more equitable society have helped guide this journey thus far as a "Dreamer", which culminated with receiving Permanent Residency in 2019 after having DACA and being a DACAmented scholar for many years. Having experienced the challenges of having these constraints, I seek to add similar atypical considerations to my work and research to find new dynamics.

Prior to my doctoral studies, I received my Bachelor’s Degree in Economics with a Mathematics Minor at Montclair State University where I graduated as the convocation speaker for the Feliciano School of Business; I am deeply grateful for the guidance and support from my advisors, Dr. Vidya Atal and Dr. Ram S. Dubey as well as the Financial Aid office and director, all whose help made my undergraduate education possible.

As I have alluded to my work outside of my academic career, I have been involved in economic consulting for the United Nations Association of El Salvador, marketing mix (econometric) modeling and strategy analysis, start-up business strategy, and in enterprise & financial risk management research.

An additional note to those of Latinx background: please reach out if you wish to talk about professional development, research, underepresented minorities in higher education, and/or Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA). I share my own experiences here.

Mardoqueo Arteaga
