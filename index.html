Welcome!

I'm Mardoqueo, or Marc, a third year PhD student in Economics at Fordham University where I also serve as the Graduate Student Coordinator for the Department of Economics. My research interests lie in the nexus between empirical macro-finance, behavioral economics, and monetary economics, with a special focus in heterogeneity and modeling expectations formation.

My name is pronounced mar-doe-KEH-oh ar-TEH-aga (originating from Marduk, the patron deity of Babylon); read more about me. This site is a work in progress!

Research

DSGE Modeling with Cognitive Discounting

Work in progress Email me for a draft
The focus of this paper is to estimate the fit of a DSGE model with a bounded rationality assumption to US economic data. Bounded rationality is in the form of cognitive discounting, or “myopia”, which quantifies how people pay less attention to events occurring further in the future.

Projects

For most of my projects, I resort to a toolkit that involves Python and Julia (for computation, web-scraping, etc.), R (for data analysis), Matlab (for any kind of structural economics), and the occasional Stata.
