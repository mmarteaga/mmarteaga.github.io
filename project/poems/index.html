Selected Poetry

Mardoqueo Arteaga
All work my own. Please send me your thoughts!

The Resistant Pyre

Thursday night, or to be direct, a time that caused me to reflect
An intimate moment with my sanity hanging from a wire.
Find me placid, partly pleasing, with my hand a stress ball squeezing,  
Awake in bed, seldom sneezing, easing back from feeling dire.
“The sickness leaves me,” I observed, “teasing back from feeling dire.”
Still, within me, burning fire.

As I tried to douse my spirit, wanting bliss or being near it,
Her eyes appeared, as if to fear it, fear my leaving from her mire.
My malady was of the heart - I should have known that, were I smart,
Yet now each thought makes up a part - part of a resistant pyre.
The guilt I feel deftly prevents me from tearing down this pyre,
Battling me like counter-fire.

And her spurious scent unsettles my senses like metals
Rubbing my skin - numbing my logic with how she takes me higher.
“No more of that,” I whisper, “at last, become her resistor.
A new future captivates bringing me back from feeling dire.
The bright future captivates bringing me back from feeling dire.”
She’s not it - there’s no more fire.

Time's Promise

What better use for words than to inspire;
It is not by chance desire rhymes with fire.
Quickly think to the lessons of the past
And note that the struggle surely won't last. 
Then, waking with the odds and ends 
Of dreams, we can justly say 
"Let us fall into our future 
In the same quite astonished way." 
For time, who holds us caught 
In her arms, has one secret to tell: 
Though uncertain today, 
The future will surely love us as well.

Gods and Vanity

The hours we spoke, oblivious to time, 
were ticking away the evening in rhyme;
At subtle pace, you wrought my spirit free
To counter you in poetry. 

Ambrosia tastes of figs and wine apart; 
Who gave you power it employs
to vainly rob me of my poise,
And, through grace, let you earn my art?

Willing Hostage

Permit me an outlandish truth to share: 
In the heart affairs, we're all magicians
Masters of shining a lantern for visions
That often cover the truth with its glare.

Under the surface, just below the waves, 
Our spirit, reflected, shows what it craves. 
How oft our illusions end as our plight 
When we had so readily cast that first light.

Chandlery Duet

Their moments of meeting are always the same
His smiling embrace as she utters his name.
The pleasantries given as a genial hook
Catch the gist of her question, ``So how do I look?''
She seeks reassurance against an uneasy shame:
Her youthfulness gone and loves that no longer lay claim.
Her casual question was all that it took
To press her vast love life in one slender book.
So time after time they meet resolutely the same;
He is her taper and she is his flickering flame.

What Once Was

Why is it that each time he said good-bye,
Your searching eyes would ask, “Is this the last?”
He heard you with a smile and then a sigh;
You both mutually craved that distant past
When he stood always ready to comply
On any weekday, bright or overcast,
With every sudden, fanciful desire
Where your impassioned ways scorched hot like fire.

Now you and he live continents apart,
But share the sad constraints of time and space.
Beware that when you let the yearnings start
And completely give them a pride of place,
They will not cease to stir your restless heart
And make of him a half-remembered face.
Just as the darkness sees the dawn arise,
So too the sun awaits the dusk, then dies.

So too the sun awaits the dusk, then dies.

The Other Half 

	  
While half the world is fighting, 
The other strains at peace.
While half the world is starving, 
The other half’s obese.
 
If half the world is drowning, 
The other half’s in drought.
While half the world’s agnostic, 
The other half’s devout.
 
While half the world’s impoverished, 
The other’s overrich.
Some dress in lavish clothing,
While some don’t wear a stitch.
 
While half the world’s straight-shooters,
The other’s shades of gay;
It’s educated guesswork
What gender’s on display.
 
We wake up every morning
To life that’s fifty-fifty,
Where nothing’s ever constant
But dubious and shifty.
 
In each contrasting instance
Diversity’s so brisk,
It can’t be just pure rumor
That Democracy’s at risk.

Nonfiction

They say that all futures come to a crawl.
That origins set are once and for all.
But where are they now, the former faces,
when surely that time comes and erases?

No singular image stays just the same;
The page has been turned with no one to blame.
Trust and care are just the tips of our range,
But life's one permanence seems to be change.

On Friendship

Friendship, what do we sum up in that one word?
What virtue of heart and spirit, indifferent at last to all but character, 
lives in those two syllables? We cling to it as the heir to the throne
of our affections with steadfast loyalty. 

What unspoken bonding at the outset accounts for its depth?
Is it the rush of recognition given in a phrase or gesture,
or is it conferred by a glance that permits a glimpse of our complete souls?

No matter the origin, we find joy in the riches dispensed over the course
of our lifetime: the companionable silences without any discomfort,
the critiques born of esteem, the disinterested caring without a hidden agenda.

It is a wholly communion. Many composing a solitude. Warmth from our mutual fire.
