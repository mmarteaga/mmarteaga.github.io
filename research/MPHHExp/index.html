Inflation Uncertainty and Monetary Policy Announcements

Mardoqueo Arteaga
Work in Progress, 2020

Introduction

This paper studies the transmission of monetary policy to household inflation expectations and explores specifications that can capture the true effect of monetary policy shocks. This effect continues being of interest to central bankers and policymakers as households in the aggregate are an important driver of economic activity. Monetary policy relies on well-anchored inflation expectations and having deeper understanding of how they are formed over time can aid forecasting. The analysis is possible using the Survey of Consumer Expectations by the Federal Reserve Bank of New York.

In order to account for potential joint effects with the exogenous monetary policy shocks and to address the foregoing patterns, I regress the inflation expectations on the lagged shocks and individual characteristics. \[ E^h Infl_{t} = \beta_{0} + \beta_{1} MP_{t-1} + \beta_{2} \mathbf{X}_{h,t} + \lambda_{h} + \epsilon_{h,t} \]

where the dependent variable, \(E^h Infl_{t}\), takes on the form of the \(h\) household's median point forecast for inflation expectations at the 12- and 36-month horizon as well as the month over month adjustment for both forecasts (a total of four distinct dependent variables whose value is derived at the month of the survey \(t\)).

The lagged monetary policy shock \(MP_{t-1}\) consists of the Federal Funds Shock and the Policy News Shock. A vector of controls is also included that contains the aforementioned variables and are collected from the household $h$ at the month of the survey \(t\). \(\lambda_h\) accounts for household fixed effects. \(\beta_1\) is the coefficient of interest and will represent the average impact the monetary policy shock has on the dependent variables of household inflation expectations. An additional exploration will include heterogeneous impacts of monetary policy by age and take on the form \[ E^h Infl_{t} = \beta_{0} + \beta_{1} MP_{t-1}\times Age_{h} + Age_{h} + \beta_{2} \mathbf{X}_{h,t} + \lambda_{h} + \epsilon_{h,t} \]
